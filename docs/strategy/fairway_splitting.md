# Current Method for Fairway Splitting and Integration

This document outlines the methodology used in the `fis-crawler` for schematizing lock and bridge structures into the fairway network graph.

## Current Method for Fairway Splitting (UTM-based Splicing)

The fundamental operation for inserting a structure (lock or bridge) into a fairway is defined in `fis.dropins.splicing` using the `FairwaySplicer` class. This approach replaces the legacy `fis.utils.process_fairway_geometry` (EPSG:28992) with a dynamically localized UTM projection for higher precision across regions.

**Method and Parameters:**
1. **Projection:** The fairway linestring and structure centroids are projected onto a dynamically estimated UTM coordinate reference system (`estimate_utm_crs()`) to find accurate distances along the fairway (`projected_dist`).
2. **Buffer Calculation (`buffer_before` and `buffer_after`):**
   - For locks, the splicing extent is determined by calculating the minimum and maximum projections of all chamber geometries along the fairway section.
   - A padding buffer (e.g., 50 meters) is added to create `buffer_before` and `buffer_after`, allowing asymmetric cutting that perfectly encapsulates staggered or non-aligned chambers.
3. **Line Splicing:** The fairway linestring is cut into segments using `substring()`, explicitly avoiding overlaps between structures and preventing negative lengths.
4. **Node Generation:** The cut points generate new nodes, assigning standard `_split` and `_merge` designations relative to the digitized direction of the fairway geometry.

## Inserting Locks

Locks are processed in `fis.lock.core` and `fis.lock.graph`.

### Inserting a lock with a single chamber
- The `split` and `merge` cut points generated by the fairway splicing logic become the topological `lock_{id}_split` and `lock_{id}_merge` nodes.
- `find_chamber_doors` calculates the nearest points on the chamber geometry to the split and merge points, designating them as `chamber_start` and `chamber_end`.
- Three sequential edges are generated to route traffic through the lock:
  1. **Approach:** `lock_split` -> `chamber_start`
  2. **Route:** `chamber_start` -> `chamber_end`
  3. **Exit:** `chamber_end` -> `lock_merge`

### Inserting a lock with two chambers (or complex structures)
- The structure uses `buffer_before` and `buffer_after` bounds that encompass all constituent chambers.
- All chambers share the **same** `lock_{id}_split` and `lock_{id}_merge` nodes on a given section.
- If chambers are not aligned (e.g., one starts earlier), the approach and exit edges dynamically connect the global split/merge nodes to the individual staggered `chamber_start` and `chamber_end` nodes.
- The graph naturally branches at the `split` node, routes through parallel chamber edges, and converges at the `merge` node.

## Inserting Bridges

Bridges are processed using a similar paradigm in `fis.bridge.core` and `fis.bridge.graph`.

### Inserting a bridge with one opening
- The fairway is spliced based on the bridge's geometric footprint, generating a `bridge_{id}_split` and `bridge_{id}_merge` node.
- The opening geometry's extreme points relative to the fairway direction become `opening_{id}_start` and `opening_{id}_end`.
- Three edges are generated:
  1. `bridge_split` -> `opening_start`
  2. `opening_passage`: `opening_start` -> `opening_end`
  3. `opening_end` -> `bridge_merge`

### Inserting a bridge with two openings
- The structure shares a single `bridge_{id}_split` and `bridge_{id}_merge` node.
- The graph branches at the `split` node into multiple parallel `opening_passage` edges (one for each physical opening) and converges back at the `merge` node.

## Inserting Locks with Bridges (Embedded Bridges)

Bridges that physically intersect a lock chamber or fall very close to it on the *same fairway section* are handled by `fis.dropins.embedded`. Instead of splitting the fairway independently, the bridge is injected into the lock's internal routing graph.

### Inserting a bridge over a lock chamber
- `identify_embedded_structures` rigorously checks for intersection or strict topological proximity (sharing the same section/fairway).
- If a bridge aligns with a chamber edge (e.g., `chamber_route`, `chamber_exit`), the original chamber edge is removed.
- `_splice_edge_at_point` cuts the edge at the opening's projected distance.
- The opening's `start` and `end` nodes are spliced directly into the middle of the chamber edge.
  - **Topology:** `chamber_start` -> `opening_start` -> `opening_end` -> `chamber_end`

## Complex Situations and Fixes

The `fis-crawler` now robustly handles highly complex scenarios via UTM splicing and branch-aware integration.

### Oranjesluizen (Multi-Branch Lock Complexes)
- **Challenge:** Multiple locks in a complex span separate, distinct physical branches of a fairway.
- **Solution:** The graph correctly handles this by evaluating each lock chamber against its topologically closest section. Split and merge points are created specific to each section (e.g., `lock_50750_59275645_split`), ensuring branches are strictly preserved without creating synthetic cross-branch approaches.

### Sluis Weurt (Multi-Section, Embedded Junctions)
- **Challenge:** A lock footprint completely consumes a pre-existing fairway junction node (e.g., `8864190`), and the chamber routing must cross from one section to another.
- **Solution:** The `_handle_consumed_junctions` logic identifies when a structure cut consumes an existing node and unifies the generated `split` or `merge` node with the original junction ID. This guarantees continuous connectivity through the lock, avoiding dead ends or self-loops, and allowing routing from `chamber_start` through the internal junction to `chamber_end`.
